A Journey Through Time

Matcha has a deep-rooted history, originating from China and flourishing in Japan, where it became a central part of the Japanese tea ceremony.

Matcha in Ancient China

The earliest forms of powdered tea date back to the Tang Dynasty (618-907 AD), where tea leaves were steamed and compressed into bricks for storage and transportation.

Zen Buddhism & The Japanese Tea Ceremony

In the 12th century, a Japanese Buddhist monk named Eisai brought powdered tea to Japan, integrating it into Zen Buddhist rituals.

Matcha in the Modern World

Today, matcha has transcended its traditional roots to become a global phenomenon, enjoyed in everything from lattes to desserts.

Matcha Popularity

Matcha has just been recently popular in the United States.
