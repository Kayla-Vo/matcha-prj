How Matcha is Made

Cultivation and Shade-Growing

Matcha plants are grown under shade for three weeks, which enhances their chlorophyll content, giving matcha its vibrant green color.

Harvesting and Processing

Only the youngest tea leaves are hand-picked. The stems and veins are removed before the leaves are steamed to preserve nutrients.

Stone Grinding

The dried leaves are ground into a fine powder using traditional granite stone mills, a slow process that enhances texture and flavor.
